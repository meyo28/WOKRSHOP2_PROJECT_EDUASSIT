Artificial intelligence has significantly changed the educational landscape, creating new opportunities for both students and teachers. AI-driven learning platforms can track student progress and modify educational content to suit individual learning styles. This adaptive approach ensures that struggling students receive additional support while advanced learners can progress at their own pace. Automated grading systems save educators significant time, allowing them to dedicate more energy to direct student engagement. Despite these benefits, we must carefully consider the ethical challenges, including student data protection, algorithmic fairness, and maintaining meaningful human interaction. The ideal educational model will likely combine AI efficiency with human expertise, where teachers mentor students while AI handles repetitive tasks. Educational institutions need to create comprehensive policies for ethical AI use that safeguard student interests.
