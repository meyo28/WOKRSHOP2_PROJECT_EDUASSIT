TASK 2: ARGUMENTATIVE SPEECH SCRIPT (3 MINUTES)
INTRODUCTION (30 seconds)
Good morning, everyone. Today, I want to ask you a question.

Have you ever studied for hours but still forgotten everything during the exam? Or have you seen a friend who studies less but gets better grades?

My topic today is: Which is better – study hard or study smart?

Many people believe that studying hard is the only way to succeed. But I disagree. I strongly believe that studying smart is better than studying hard.

Let me explain why with three main reasons.

BODY – ARGUMENT 1: Saves Time (45 seconds)
First, studying smart saves time.

Studying hard means reading the same chapter again and again for hours. But studying smart means using effective techniques like summarizing, creating mind maps, or practicing past exam questions.

For example, imagine two students. One reads a textbook for five hours without any plan. The other spends two hours making notes and answering practice questions. Who learns more? The second student.

Studying smart helps you learn more in less time. This gives you extra time for rest, hobbies, or other subjects.

BODY – ARGUMENT 2: Reduces Stress (45 seconds)
Second, studying smart reduces stress.

When you study hard but don't see results, you feel frustrated and tired. This can lead to burnout, anxiety, and even giving up.

But when you study smart, you feel confident because your methods work. You see progress. You sleep better. You feel ready for exams.

Think about it. Would you rather study ten hours feeling lost, or study four hours feeling focused? Studying smart keeps your mind healthy and positive.

BODY – ARGUMENT 3: Improves Long-Term Memory (45 seconds)
Third, studying smart improves long-term memory.

Studying hard often uses memorization. You repeat facts until you remember them. But after the exam, you forget everything.

Studying smart uses methods like active recall and spaced repetition. You test yourself. You review information over several days. This helps your brain store knowledge for a long time.

For example, using flashcards or teaching a friend helps you understand deeply, not just memorize. This knowledge stays with you for years, not just for one exam.

COUNTER-ARGUMENT (15 seconds)
Some people might say, "But hard work is important. You cannot succeed without effort."

And I agree. Effort is important. But studying smart does not mean being lazy. It means working efficiently. You still work hard – but you work smart too.

CONCLUSION (30 seconds)
In conclusion, studying smart is better than studying hard for three reasons.

First, it saves time. Second, it reduces stress. Third, it improves long-term memory.

Does this mean we should stop working hard? No. But we should stop working blindly. We should work with a plan, with good methods, and with confidence.

So next time you study, ask yourself: Am I studying hard, or am I studying smart?

Thank you for listening.


POSSIBLE Q&A QUESTIONS & ANSWERS
Be ready for these two questions from the audience:

Q1: "Don't you think some subjects require studying hard, like medicine or law?"

A: That is a good question. For difficult subjects, you still need to study hard. But studying smart helps you organize information better. For example, medical students use flashcards and diagrams to remember body parts. That is studying smart. So both are important, but smart methods make hard work more effective.

Q2: "What is one study smart technique you personally use?"

*A: I use the Pomodoro technique. I study for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. This helps me focus without feeling tired. I also write short summaries after each chapter instead of reading everything again.*
